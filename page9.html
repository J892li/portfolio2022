Why we are doing this project? 

This project is under NDA, so I cannot share some of the content publicly. 

The VOTR project is the Canada’s first Métis mixed reality game combines traditional knowledge with new media, weaving storytelling, cultural practices, and language into an evolving gameplay with rich, cultural mechanics. Key features include:harvesting, crafting and combat  

First, the process 

This is a four-month project with the goal of providing our clients with a working Unity prototype. During those four months, our team completed 4 sprints, each consisted of 2 weeks. 

The first 3 sprints is focused game design for the 3 main features (Combat, Harvest, Craft), yet we used the flow of story-boarding - prototyping - developing for each sprints. 

By the end of the project, we completed 9 concepts( we picked one for each system), and we combined the selected ideas to make the ultimate product. 

Image Description

Challenges 1 - How to avoid stepping on cultural misunderstandings in design

It's fascinating to use cultural elements in game design. Especially this project is the Canada’s first Métis mixed reality game combines traditional knowledge with new media. So the challenge is straightforward here, how can we avoid the mistake, and how can we design with respect? 

Solution:

Our team carefully read the onscreen protocol for the use of indigenous cultural elements. We communicate about the norms to make sure we don't make stupid mistakes. Secondly, we also watched the TV series with the production team to understand more behind-the-scenes stories and backgrounds, to align our game with the main story. 

Image Description

Challenge 2: How to design for different system in a holistic way？ 

This project contains 3 main features, which is the 3 main system in the game. It is certainly easier for team to process by doing sprints and separated the system one by one. Yet, the challenge is, after we designed for individual system, how can we combined the selected ideas into one prototype? 

Solution: 
When our team are delivering concepts to our client team, we make sure their is matched visual design and interaction style. Yet, we always design for holistic experience, and consistently asking the team: what is the final experiences we are trying to deliver, and why we are doing this design？We review our sprint result and testing results, then deliver the final solutions to our client.  
 

Team Punch and kick 

Challenge 3: Team conflict and the way out 

Development can be a hard and frustrating job, but if  the project manager can help with removing blocker, it will be another story.
 
Our artist is heavy loaded with asset creation duty in the middle of the project, and our team is having conflict about the design for the game.  The challenge is, for me, how to help the team perform better? 

Solution:
The right feedback, the right planning. 
If the team worked on a heavy sprint last week, It might be depressing to point out that the previous work need to be redo. 
The solution is to respect the work we made, and let the team know that why we are making changes. I will try to split the tasks to smaller pieces and make sure there this is something we all agreed to change, so the team is not doing work outside of the scope.
